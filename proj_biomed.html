    Artificial and Augmented sensing for humans and humanoids - Summer School

    This experience consisted in a series of lectures and practical activities on the basis of human somatosensation and the computational and technological methods to integrate sensing in robots.


    The syllabus included:
    • Somatosensory process mechanisms at the physical, behavioural, biomechanical, and neural level, including implications for motor control and learning.
    • Understanding the artificial tactile-proprioceptive sensing and feedback.
    • Laboratories about the following topics: neurorehabilitation, prosthetics and sensory restoration, and robotics (humanoids and robot-assisted surgery).
