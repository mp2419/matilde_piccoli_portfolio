  5. Mars Rover Project

    May-June 2021, Imperial College London

    This interdisciplinary project consisted in the design and implemention of a Rover able to map the surroundings, detect objects and autonomously moving to specific targets. The Embedded system consisted of five main units: Energy, Drive, Vision, Control and Communication.
    I was leading the implementation and testing processes of the Drive Unit, responsible for the Rover movements, and the intergration with the Power and Control Unit.
    This role involved working with optical sensors, motors, an Arduino, and an SMPS (Switched-Mode Power Supply).
    The moving algorithms in autonomous and manual modes were implemented in C++ and PID (Proportional Integral Derivative) controller was ccreated for the error correction. All the features were implemented beyond the given requirements thanks to accurate design methodology and a collaborative environment.
    The team recieved an Upper 1st Class and the Award of Best Project of the Year.
