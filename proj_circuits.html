Oct-Dec 2021, Imperial College London

    CPU Design - Project

    Following a series of lectures and laboratories on Digital Electronics and Computer Architecture, my team and I decided to implemenet our own CPU and ISA from scratch, to understand in details how the design works.
    Our goal was to create an architecture taylored to tackle specific digital electronics tasks in an time and power efficient way.
    My results were the following:

    • Designed and tested the basic building of our CPU using QuartusPrime and Verilog;
    • Implemented an algorithm to iterate through linked-lists and a pseudo-random number generator.
    • Delivered a fully working CPU, with a complete set of instructions, its own assembly language and indirect addressing, and a technical report within the deadlines.
    • Managed the scheduling and role division of the group developing leadership skills.
